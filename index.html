Urban Mobility Explorer

A public-facing research interface for exploring neighbourhood and city-level patterns in urban mobility, belonging, and trust.

This explorer presents selected descriptive findings from the Mobility, Temporality, and Africa’s Future Politics research project. The survey was conducted in 2021 across nine neighbourhoods in Accra, Nairobi, and Johannesburg, with a total sample of 1,644 respondents.

The interface allows users to explore neighbourhood and city-level distributions for key variables related to belonging, perceived outsider status, trust in local institutions, mobility expectations, and demographic structure.

Why this matters. Urban mobility is not only about movement between places. It also shapes how people experience belonging, trust institutions, and imagine their future in the city. Making these patterns visible helps researchers, policymakers, and practitioners better understand how urban residents navigate complex social environments.
