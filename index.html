What this is

A comparative urban data resource on mobility, belonging, and city life

This explorer presents selected descriptive findings from the Mobility, Temporality, and Africa’s Future Politics project. It is designed to make neighbourhood- and city-level survey patterns easier to inspect, compare, teach, and reuse.

The public release focuses on selected variables related to belonging, perceived outsider status, institutional trust, mobility expectations, and demographic structure. The dataset is best read as a comparative urban research resource, not as a nationally representative survey archive.

Interpretation note

Use the patterns carefully

The study was designed to compare selected migrant-rich urban neighbourhoods, not to represent entire cities or national populations. The data are therefore best used for descriptive and exploratory analysis.

Its value lies in structured comparison: how belonging, trust, mobility histories, and future expectations vary across different urban settings.
